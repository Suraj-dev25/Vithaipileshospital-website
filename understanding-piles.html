Understanding Piles

What are Piles?

Imagine your anal canal as a network of veins. Piles are swollen and enlarged versions of these veins, similar to varicose veins in the legs. Due to increased pressure in the area, these veins become stretched and irritated, often requiring effective piles treatment in PCMC, Pune at Vithai Piles Clinic to alleviate discomfort.

What Causes Piles?

Several factors can contribute to the development of piles, including:

  • Straining During Bowel Movements: Whether it's due to constipation or simply pushing too hard, straining puts extra pressure on your anal veins
  • Chronic Constipation: Hard stools can be difficult to pass, leading to straining and irritation.
  • Sitting for Long Periods: This applies to both sitting on the toilet and prolonged sitting. Gravity plays a role, increasing pressure on the anal veins.
  • Pregnancy: The growing uterus in pregnancy puts pressure on the pelvic area, including the veins around the anus.
  • Diet Low in Fiber: Fiber helps keep stools soft and bulky, making them easier to pass. A lack of fiber can lead to constipation and straining.
  • Lifting Heavy Objects: Regularly lifting heavy weights can put additional strain on your anal veins.

Symptoms of Piles

Piles can manifest in several ways, depending on their severity. Here are some common symptoms:

  • Bleeding: Bright red blood on toilet paper or in the toilet bowl is a telltale sign of piles.
  • Pain or Discomfort: You might experience a burning or stinging sensation around the anus, especially during bowel movements.
  • Itching: The irritated area can become itchy and uncomfortable.
  • Protrusion: In some cases, the hemorrhoids may protrude outside the anus.

Grades of Piles

Piles are categorized based on their severity:

  • Grade I: These piles remain inside the anus and usually cause no symptoms.
  • Grade II: They protrude during bowel movements but return on their own.
  • Grade III: These piles protrude and require manual pushing back into the anus.
  • Grade IV: They protrude permanently and cannot be pushed back in.

Finding Relief from Piles

The good news is that piles are a treatable condition. Depending on the severity, your doctor might recommend:

  • Lifestyle Changes: The first steps are to increase fiber intake, stay hydrated, and avoid straining during bowel movements.
  • Sitz Baths: Soaking in a warm bath can provide soothing relief.
  • Over-the-counter Medications: Creams and ointments can help reduce inflammation and discomfort.
  • Minimally Invasive Procedures: Rubber band ligation and sclerotherapy are standard options for internal piles.
  • Surgery: In severe cases, surgery may be necessary to remove the hemorrhoids.

Types of Piles Treatment

Piles, also known as hemorrhoids, can be treated in a variety of ways based on their severity and symptoms. Treatment options range from simple lifestyle changes to surgical procedures. Here's a brief summary of the various therapies available:

Nonsurgical Procedure

These minimally invasive procedures are utilized for internal piles that do not respond to conservative approaches.

  • Rubber Band Ligation: A little rubber band is wrapped around the base of the pile, cutting off the blood supply and causing it to shrink and fall off within a week.
  • Sclerotherapy: It involves injecting a chemical solution into the pile to shrink it by hardening the veins.
  • Coagulation therapy: It involves hardening and shrinking piles with heat, laser, or infrared light.

Surgical treatments

Surgery is often reserved for severe or persistent cases of piles (Grade III and IV).

  • Hemorrhoidectomy: It is the surgical removal of piles. This is really effective; however, it may take a longer recovery time.
  • Stapled Hemorrhoidopexy (PPH): A surgical stapler is used to relocate and fasten the piles back inside the rectum, preventing blood flow and reducing their size.
  • Laser treatment: It is a minimally invasive procedure that employs a laser to remove or decrease heaps. This procedure is rapid, precise, and provides a faster recovery

Ayurveda & Natural Treatments

In some circumstances, individuals choose alternate treatments

  • Ayurvedic Remedies: Herbal medicines and therapies, such as Triphala or neem leaf baths, can be used to alleviate inflammation.
  • Yoga and exercise: Certain poses, such as Vajrasana or Pawanmuktasana, might aid digestion and prevent constipation.
